MAZAGRAN


About

What could be better than a good old fashioned cup of coffee? We have one idea! Wait until you try mazagran, a European coffee drink made with lemon and sugar. It’s sometimes called the original iced coffee — and you may never go back to your previous drinks! Mazagran is a cold espresso drink that originally comes from Algeria, though it’s now much more popular in Portugal. It’s essentially coffee lemonade, combining citrusy acidity with the bold flavor of espresso. The lemon juice was originally added to improve the flavor of low-end coffee beans. But don’t hesitate to use your pricier beans — this tasty drink won’t ruin their flavor.


Recipe

Ingredients

  • Fresh espresso or strong coffee
  • Lemon
  • White sugar or honey
  • Ice

Instructions

  1. Fill the glass with ice — or, for a stronger drink, use coffee ice cubes.
  2. Using your preferred espresso maker, pull a shot. If you prefer, you can use strong coffee instead, though you may want to adjust the ratios. We used the Wacaco Minipresso, an affordable portable espresso maker, for this guide.
  3. Mix white sugar, honey, or vanilla extract into the hot coffee. Then let the mixture cool for a few minutes.
  4. Pour the espresso mixture on top of the ice.
  5. Depending on how much you like sour flavors, you may want to add more or less lemon juice. We recommend squeezing in half a lemon (about 1 tablespoon of juice). Then stir, taste, and adjust if needed!
  6. If you’d like, add a slice of lemon as a garnish. Now you’re ready to enjoy your iced lemon coffee!
