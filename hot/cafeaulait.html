CAFE AU LAIT


About

“Cafe au lait” is French for “coffee with milk.” The drink originated in France and made with drip coffee (not espresso) and steamed milk. It’s usually made with equal parts coffee and milk but there isn’t a set rule with the ratio of coffee to milk. Cafe au laits are one of the simplest coffee shop drinks to make at home, since no espresso is required. A popular part of New Orleans culture, cafe au laits are sometimes made with chicory.


Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons coffee
  • ⅔ cup water
  • ⅔ cup milk

Instructions

  1. Grind coffee (or use pre-ground coffee) and brew in a coffee machine.
  2. To froth milk using a French press, heat milk on the stovetop (simmer) or in a microwave then pour it into a French press. Use the plunger to pump warm milk until it doubles in volume. The quickest and best way to froth milk at home is to use an electric milk frother which heats and froths at the same time.
  3. Pour coffee into a cup. Add frothed milk. The drink is equal parts coffee and milk but you can adjust it to your taste.
