RISTRETTO


About

A ristretto is a shot of espresso made with less water. It’s a smaller drink than an espresso since it’s more concentrated, making it richer and slightly sweeter. One of the reasons a ristretto shot is sweeter is because as water pushes through espresso grounds, different compounds dissolve at different speeds. Sweet and acidic flavors dissolve first, and bitter compounds dissolve later. “Ristretto” means “restricted” or “shortened” in Italian, which refers to restricting the amount of water the espresso machine pushes through to make the drink. The biggest indicator of a ristretto shot is the volume of the shot. A ristretto is around 15-20 ml and an espresso is 25-35 ml in drink volume.


Recipe

Ingredients

  • 15-18 grams ground coffee beans
  • Filtered water

Instructions

  1. Remove portafiter from espresso machine and run the water through the grouphead to remove any leftover coffee grounds.
  2. Grind and fill dry portafilter with finely ground coffee.
  3. Level and tamp filled portafilter. Clean loose ground coffee off the portafilter.
  4. Put portafilter into the grouphead and pull ristretto shots. There should be less water in a ristretto shot so pull a shorter shot than you would an espresso. The drink should be about ½ an ounce.
  5. Place cup under the portafilter.
  6. After pulling shots of ristretto, remove the portafilter and flush grouphead.


Source: Coffee at Three
